Biography

Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O'Connel (born December 18, 2001) is an American singer and songwriter. She first gained public attention in 2015 with her brother Finneas O'Connell, with whom she collaborates on music and live shows. In 2017, she released her debut extended play (EP), Don't Smile at Me. Commercially sucessful, it reached the top 15 of record charts in numerous countries, including the US,UK, Canada, and Australia.

Billie Eilish has received multiple accolades, including nine Grammy Awards, two American Music Awards, twenty Guinness World Records, seven MTV Video and lots of more. She is the daughter of actress and teacher Maggie Baird and actor Patrick O'Connell, both of whom are also musicians and work on Billie's tours. Her middle name "Eilish" was originally meant to be her first name, while Pirate was to have been her middle name. She was named Billie after the death of her grandfather.

Billie grew up listening to the Beatles, Justin Bieber, Green Day, the 1975, Arctic Monkeys, Linkin Park, Lana Del Rey. She has said that stumbling upon Aurora's "Runaway" on Youtube inspired her to pursue a music career. Hip Hop is her favorite genre and biggest inspiration.
